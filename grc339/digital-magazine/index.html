Strange Foods in San Francisco

San Francisco is a city that's always prided itself on being unusual. One of the best ways to see the city and experience this yourself is to eat your way through it. Here are just some of San Francisco's most unique dining experiences.

Andrew Zimmern is notorious for traveling the world eating weird dishes. From putrefied shark in Iceland to rectum sausage in Kazakhstan, he’s digested his fair share of bizarre and unappealing foods. On a trip to San Francisco, Andrew ate his way around our plethora of international restaurants, sampling an array of strange delicacies. We’re rounding up a few of the places he dined and some of the weird things he feasted on, plus adding a few of our own that we think the food pro should try on his next trip here.

San Francisco is definitely abnormal when compared to the average American city, so it’s no surprise it’s also home to a number of strange places to dine. Check out this list of the weirdest restaurants in San Francisco and make your next dining experience an eccentric one.

San Francisco has been a renowned restaurant town almost from its birth. Today’s eateries are probably unsurpassed in the quality of their food, but there is one area in which they fall lamentably short of their predecessors: weirdness.

We take our food very seriously in San Francisco, but we also like to have a little fun with it. Pairing foods and ingredients to make strange food combinations may just be a Bay Area pastime. San Franciscans love their mouthwatering breads, seafood, burritos, ice cream, and fries, all with a truly local, truly awesome spin. And for better or worse, we have no qualms about standing in line or waking up early for them.

San Francisco Bay Area is not only home to some of the most stunning landmarks in the country, it’s also home to dozens of weird restaurants that offer a variety of delectable dishes. From a ‘dining in the dark’ restaurant to a café littered with troll dolls and dildos, these weird restaurants in the Bay Area will surely make a tourist’s dining experience an eccentric one.
